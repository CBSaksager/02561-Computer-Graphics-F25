Explanation of settings:
  • Texture Edge: Defines how texture coordinates outside the range [0, 1] are handled.
      "Clamp-to-edge" will stretch the edge pixels, while "Repeat" will just start over.
  • Minificantio Filter: Defines how a texture is showed when it is smaller on screen than its original size.
    • "Nearest" will use the nearst texel. This creates a pixelated look.
    • "Linear" will interpolate between the nearest texels. This creates a smoother look but can be blurry.
    Linear filtering has little effect on minification issues without mipmapping.
  • Magnification Filter: Defines how a texture is showed when it is bigger on screen than its original size.
      "Nearest" and "Linear" works the same way as for minification.
  • Mipmap Filter: Controls how the mipmap levels are blended when rendering.
    • "Nearest" will select the closest mipmap level without blending.
    • "Linear" will blend between the two closest mipmap levels for smoother transitions.
